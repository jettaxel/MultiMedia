Rotoscoping Project

Frame-by-frame animation through digital tracing

Rotoscoping is a traditional animation technique where animators trace over live-action footage frame by frame to create realistic motion. This project explores the meticulous process of digital rotoscoping, combining traditional animation principles with modern digital tools.

Through this 12-week journey, I documented every step of the rotoscoping process, from initial footage analysis to final animation refinement.

Tools Used

  • Premiere Pro / After Effects: For syncing, compiling, and exporting the animation with audio if needed.
  • Photoshop: Cleaning up frames and adjusting color/line work if necessary.
  • DaVinci Resolve: Cutting and color grading the final video as needed.
  • Reference Footage: Live-action dance video used as the primary animation base.
  • MP4 Video Format: Used throughout to maintain compatibility and playback performance.
  • Capcut: Finishing touches and effects.

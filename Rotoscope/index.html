Rotoscoping Project

Frame-by-frame animation through digital tracing

Rotoscoping is a traditional animation technique where animators trace over live-action footage frame by frame to create realistic motion. This project explores the meticulous process of digital rotoscoping, combining traditional animation principles with modern digital tools.

Through this 12-week journey, I documented every step of the rotoscoping process, from initial footage analysis to final animation refinement.

Tools Used

  • Premiere Pro / After Effects: Used for syncing the animation with audio, compiling various media elements into a cohesive sequence, and exporting the final animation. After Effects is also utilized for complex motion graphics and visual effects work when needed.
  • After Effects Roto Brush: A key tool used to isolate subjects from the background in the reference footage. This feature allows frame-by-frame refinement for precise cutouts, enabling cleaner rotoscope work and layered compositions.
  • Photoshop: Applied for detailed frame-by-frame cleanup of the rotoscoped drawings. It's used to fix line inconsistencies, refine outlines, and adjust coloring or contrast for visual clarity and stylistic consistency.
  • DaVinci Resolve: Used during the final post-production stage for precise video cutting, transitions, and advanced color grading. This ensures that the final animation has cinematic color balance and smooth scene transitions.
  • Reference Footage: High-quality live-action video served as the foundational layer for rotoscoping. This footage was carefully selected to capture realistic motion and body dynamics, ensuring authenticity in the animation process.
  • MP4 Video Format: Maintained throughout the production pipeline for its balance between quality and performance. It ensures seamless playback, ease of editing, and compatibility across editing software and platforms.
  • Capcut: Used in the final polishing phase to add stylistic effects, on-screen text, and dynamic transitions. It provides a user-friendly interface for quick edits and enhances the visual appeal of the final video output.
